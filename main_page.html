Understanding the Technology

What actually
is AI?

AI (or Artificial Intelligence) uses large amounts of data to learn how to do things — much like how a person learns from experience, but at a scale and speed no human could match. Feed it millions of customer emails and it learns to write responses. Show it thousands of financial records and it learns to spot fraud. The more quality data it has, the smarter it becomes.

Under the hood, modern AI works by finding deep patterns in data that would be invisible to the human eye. It builds a kind of internal "model" of the world — a set of rules it has worked out for itself — and applies those rules to new situations it has never seen before.

The practical result is software that can understand natural language, make accurate predictions, automate complex decisions, and even generate entirely new content — all without being explicitly told how to do any of it. That is what makes it genuinely different from traditional software.
